GOCHUJANG ________
STIR FRY

Gochujang is a powerhouse chilli paste made from red chillies, rice flour, malt syrup and fermented soy beans. Possessing the salty depth of miso, it has a sweeter taste and is very spicy. Use it as a base for a quick stir-fry sauce.

Total Time: 30 mins Serving: 4 servings
Skill Level: Beginner Heat level: Medium but can be modified

Story

Korean restaurants are my go-to. When lockdown happened, I craved korean food alot, so i decided, with all the time i got, to learn a dish or two. I'm not very good at cooking, or have the patience for it, but this gochujang stir fry is very quick, easy to follow and very good. This dish also has a lot of variations and can be tweaked according to preference, you'll see what I mean. After making my first dish of gochujang stir fry, I was surprised of how it tasted and how cheap it is to make it compared to going to a restaurant. This may be the only korean dish i know, for now, but it helps me satisfy my Korean food cravings whenever I have them.

Process

1. In a pan, cook chosen meat about 80% and put aside.

2. In a large wok, heat 1 tbsp of oil. Stir fry the onion, garlic and ginger until softened, about 2 minutes.

3. Add another tbsp of oil to the pan and add vegetables. Keep stir frying until half cooked.

4. Add the meat and gochujang sauce mix. Stir-fry for another 3 minutes or until sauce gets thick.

Plating

Serve with steamed rice. Gochujang stir fry can be served on top of steamed rice or on the side. Garnish with a sprinkle of spring onions and sesame seeds. Enjoy while it's hot.
